What it means, to me, to be Heathen

I have been circling this point for a long time, just not realizing what was calling to me, within myself.

I left Christianity because my own experience was so destructive, repetitively so.  A major problem, for me, was the abdication of personal responsibility, replacing one's own choices about spiritual expression with the pastor or church's choices.  The individual has to take responsibility for their sins, but only in a way which keeps them under the thumb of the organization.

I read about witchcraft and Wicca when I was in my 20s.  There's a lot of information out there, and no set standard.  I was attracted to the personal power, skeptical of some of the claims, offended by some stances on morals, and, undoubtedly too close to my Christian upbringing to avoid feeling frightened that I was doing something truly evil just by reading about it.

I also dabbled in Buddhism.  For 15 months, with the encouragement of an older sister I idolized, I participated in a Zendo, and even attended a 4 day Sesshin.  I came to realize that I had, again, substituted someone else's spirituality for my own, and decided I would keep searching until I could be sure I was doing what I wanted.

For a good stretch, about 15 years or more, I participated nominally in Catholicism.  At this time, I continued to read historical books regarding the Christian religion.  My first two children were baptized in the Catholic Church, because I was persuaded by my husband's reasoning that it is part of our culture, and it would hurt his parents to not do so.  I was internally conflicted, as well, feeling it would just be easier, for the sake of getting along, if I embraced it, especially because I know so many people who are nominal, and one religion is just as good as another.  (Or so I reasoned)

I tried for a time, when my daughter was small, to go to church at least once a month.  It's very trying, to take a little kid, and not really get anything out of it yourself.  And since George absolutely refused, I quit.

In early 2003, we were in, again, a tough financial place.  I was very frustrated as a mom, trying to balance finances, motherhood, my confusion regarding religion/spirituality/ethics/intuition.  While unemployed, I began visiting websites regarding Traditional Paganism, Witchcraft, and some shamanistically oriented sites.

In May of 2003, I surprised myself entirely, by recommitting my life to Christ. (I suspect I had scared myself by looking so seriously at paganism and witchcraft.)   Shortly thereafter, I went to an Orthodox Church, and was so moved by the artwork and music I became convinced that this was the experience I was looking for, the picture that tied all the Christian pieces together for me.  It had touched on the mystical/shamanistic I thought I was looking for.

It lasted for about 3 very intense months.  During that time, I tried to be as structured as my childhood was, even compulsive.  Gradually I began to recover some emotions tied to my family, and very early childhood, before Vatican II really took effect.  I began to see that I was recreating my childhood so that I could face some issues, and heal.  I also became increasingly disturbed at the dichotomy between what I was experiencing in my own heart during Liturgical services, vs. the self-righteousness, rigidity, and arrogance of the community of believers around me.

I woke up one morning completely done with it.  And frustrated with myself.

I then embarked on the same quest, from a different angle.  I reasoned that I had often had quite remarkable self-revelations by tapping into my own subconscious mind, so I sought to alter my consciousness and get feedback from my deepest self.  I also decided to try to determine the most natural human tendencies regarding spirituality, what elements seem to be evident throughout time and space.

Ultimately I stumbled onto the Shamanism Board at Beliefnet.com.
