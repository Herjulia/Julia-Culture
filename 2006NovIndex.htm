Changing My Worldview

I am actively engaged in changing my worldview.  To me, what this means is that, as I consider or embrace a new element in my life, I look at the context in which I am changing it.  Whether philosophical, ethical, spiritual, scientific, physical, I want to take the old view or habit in its context, consider whether the new element is compatible and why. 

I don't have a laundry list.  What I do have are certain areas of angst in my life.  In the past I was trying to change my habits by simply plugging the new habits into my old worldview.  That method has really not worked for me.

About three years ago, my change in world view took on a more conscious activity.  I began to realize to what extent I was out of harmony with my own heart and soul.  It was amazing, really, as I had always thought I was quite independent. But I found I was consistently stuffing my very soul down in order to please those around me.

Herbalism:

I believe that healthcare, to be sustainable, must turn back to the individual.  We need to take responsibility for eating right, exercising, maintaining our psycho/spiritual balance, and treating ourselves for the most common, non-emergency, non-life threatening issues.  New evidence is coming forth, that the majority of medicines are in "weeds" within a few hundred feet of most people's homes.  We need to return to the old knowledge that our ancestors knew.

Permaculture:

I first heard this word on NPR, describing a backyard garden that could be left for up to 2 weeks with no intervention.  It was in the desert. 

Integrative Medicine:  this is a term referring to blending allopathic and alternative care, rather than choosing one or the other.

My first step is going to be taking some CEUs on an integrative approach to asthma.  As a respiratory therapist, it seems a good place to start, if I think I might blend my current profession with a new direction.

Asian and European Martial Arts:

I stopped studying Shotokan karate during my 12 week of pregnancy for my first child, after 6 years of study.  I occasionally do a little on my own, knowing enough to practice technique, and try to get a little conditioning.  Last Spring I took and introductory class in Medieval LongSword, after much prompting from my husband.  Definitely a great workout, and very interesting.  I joked with the Chicago Swordplay Guild members that this was where "Geek meets Jock". 

However, for my current interests, I am returning to karate.  On September 8 I just had bunion surgery, 8 weeks out, now, and I want to start Shidokan karate at the Chicago Fitness Center.

Religion/Spirituality/Atheism

My Christian upbringing put me in about every Christian context you can imagine.  I have done quite a bit of spiritual exploration, most recently, Heathenry (Asatru).

As much as I want there to be magic, answered prayer, direct contact with a god/goddess or other sort of Deity-ish thing, I cannot say I've every had any of the above.  No spiritual type experience I've ever had could be explained by anything outside my head.  I do believe much has been of value, just as far as personal growth; however, I've reached a point where I want things less complicated, and it's entirely too much work to keep trying to conjure up faith in the supernatural.
