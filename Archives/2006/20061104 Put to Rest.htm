Changing My Worldview

I am actively engaged in changing my worldview.  To me, what this means is that, as I consider or embrace a new element in my life, I look at the context in which I am changing it.  Whether philosophical, ethical, spiritual, scientific, physical, I want to take the old view or habit in its context, consider whether the new element is compatible and why. 

I don't have a laundry list.  What I do have are certain areas of angst in my life.  In the past I was trying to change my habits by simply plugging the new habits into my old worldview.  That method has really not worked for me.

About three years ago, my change in world view took on a more conscious activity.  I began to realize to what extent I was out of harmony with my own heart and soul.  It was amazing, really, as I had always thought I was quite independent. But I found I was consistently stuffing my very soul down in order to please those around me.

Permaculture:

I first heard this word on NPR, describing a backyard garden that could be left for up to 2 weeks with no intervention.  It was in the desert. 

Permaculture:  A contraction of the words "permanent" and either "agriculture" or just "culture".  The idea  all about sustainability.

Some concepts that are included or related as far as gardening goes are "Woodland gardening", "Forest gardening", "edible landscaping".

I've been wanting to implement these principles, but was living in an apartment up until last summer.  This year, we're going to plant a few annual vegetables for fun, but the BIG thing, is to design an edible landscape, and begin addressing our soil and water situation.

But this is my vision:

Although I live in a major urban center, I want to get off the grid.  Wherever possible, I want to generate our own energy, rely on our own water cycle, and generate/eat our own food.  I hope to develop a cottage industry from our produce.

Tyr

I have not begun reading too much of Tyr.  He's just sort of come to mind lately, and I've offered a few prayers to him for insight into justice.

He is either Odin's son, or that of the giant Hymir.  Originally, he was the god of battle, but made way for Odin to become the god of battle during viking times.

His attribute is a spear, and he is shown one handed, his right having been bitten off when he gave it in pledge to the Fenris Wolf, placing it in his mouth so he would submit to being leashed.

His role in Ragnarok will be to kill the hound Garm, but he will die in this battle.

He is the most bold or gods, inspiring courage and heroism in battle.

I'll link to the Tyr and Zisa (Tyr's consort) article on the Troth, for further study.
