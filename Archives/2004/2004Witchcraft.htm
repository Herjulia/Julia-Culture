I began reading a little about Wicca in the 1980's, as I was losing my enchantment with Christianity, and I stopped participating in Buddhism.  At that time, I read "Drawing Down the Moon" by Margot Adler, and "The Spiral Dance" by Starhawk.

Initially, I was just trying to "de-prejudice" myself.  After all, Christians had done a lot of damage to me, so why should I take at face value what they had to say about witches?  For a while I was interested in finding a coven, but it proved to be typical of my enthusiastic bursts.  Also, because I had spent my adolescence in a group that rivaled The Branch Davidians, I decided I just wasn't ready for groups.

In the last 5 years or so, I have had an increasing longing to adjust my lifestyle to my spirit.  I feel that we live so divorced from our intuition, and bodies, and our work usually pits us against our families.  Somewhere in the midst of this search for a more "organic" life, I was exploring permaculture, and started stumbling onto more and more sites by Pagans and Witches.

During that time, I came across the works of Robin Artisson, and saw for the first time, a connection between Native American ways of relating to nature and Spirit, and that of the Northern Europeans.  I am not saying they are one and the same!!  But the first time, I saw a primitive, natural, spirituality in witchcraft, and I grew fond of this.

Basically, Wicca is currently a variety of eclectic groups that incorporate influences from a variety of western and eastern influences.  Without a doubt, there are those who call themselves Wiccan who are deeply spiritual, and sincere.  However, this religion, or collection of religions, has been heavily marketed, and watered down, so you might have to exercise a lot of wisdom, and wade through a lot of garbage to get to the good stuff.  You might also waste a lot of money to get to the good stuff.  For myself, most of these groups are more structured than I am personally interested in.

Witchcraft is hard to nail down, and I will leave it to Mr. Artisson to explain more fully.  Basically, there is less structure, and more oral tradition.  These traditions can trace their lineage to times as far back as the 17th century, but not before.

I have been on a few discussion groups, trying to talk to witches, and I have to say, they have been hard to carry on discussions with.  It seems I have to define everything, and often I am warned about possibly offending people by the choice of this or that word.  So for now, I am putting my energy into web sites and books, and will wait to see if I ever get a straight answer on anything from a practicing witch.
