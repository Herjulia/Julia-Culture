Frigg 

I begin here, because the Wife of Odin (that's a "kenning", or another or a "poetic name for" Frigg) is a wife and mother, and much of what I already know of her is part of my own spiritual ideal.

    Overview

    Frigg is, as mentioned, Odin's wife, counterpart, equal.  She is the keeper of her house, and promotes domestic tranquility.  She knows and spins fate, but tells no one what she knows.  She is the Mother of the God Baldr, and tried to prevent his death by going throughout the world and extracting a promise from  every creature not to harm him.  But she overlooked the mistletoe, and it was through this being that Baldr is killed.  So she is a weeping mother.

    Her home is called "Fensilar", or "marsh-halls", and has many handmaidens who are also goddesses.

    In Old Norse it is Frigg, Anglo-Saxon Frige, Old High German Frija, Wagnerian Fricka.  It isn't clear to me where Frigga comes from.  I guess I'll just stick with Frigg.

   References from the Lore

    In the "Lokesanna", the trickster god Loki is giving all the gods "what for", pointing out there flaws or apparent flaws.  He accuses Frigg of infidelity with Odin's brothers.   Interestingly, she doesn't exactly refute him, but simply says "If Baldr were here, he'd make you pay". 

    In the "Grimnismal", Odin and Frigg together foster two brothers, Odin Geirroth, and Frigg Agnar.  Geirroth tricks Agnar out of his inheritance, and the two deities take sides. (No, I'm not retelling the story.  I'm referencing the Lore--for myself, for future study. I told you I had kids, right?)

Odin

This is just a little UPG (Unverified Personal Gnosis).

The first few days I began reading about Allfather, I had sort of a little talk with him.  It began with me acknowledging to him that I was sort of cowardly when it comes to thinking of interacting with him.  The "conversation" took turns that I did not expect, leading from my supposed fear of him, to realizing I was afraid of what he might evoke in myself, or that in myself which is like him.

For a couple of years now, I keep hearing inner statements that I must give in to my "Inner Witch", or that I need to give myself permission to go crazy.  The conversation was along those lines.  That I need the ecstatic in myself to be fulfilled spiritually, and that I need the berserker in myself to protect my family and keep it safe.

All the same, I doubt that I am Valhalla material.

Thor

Thor is the God of Thunder, Warder of Midgard and Asgard.  Usually not shown as sharp-witted as Odin or Loki, he is practical, and his solutions to problems show common sense.  Many places throughout Northern Europe bear his name, demonstrating how widely worshipped he was.  As God of both Thunder and Rain, he was considered a great friend to man. He is protector and hallower, having one heiti, Véurr, "he that makes sacred".

Mjollnir is his hammer,  used to fight giants, as well as hallow brides and funeral pyres.  It is widely worn by Heathens today as a sign of troth.

Big and muscular, with red beard and hair, and fiery eyes, the Thunder God drives a wagon drawn by Tanngrisnir and Tannnjoster, and is seen today as the protector of his Mother, Earth (Jord, the giantess).
